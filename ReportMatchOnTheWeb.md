### Short Instructions

This section contains general information about the Backgammon website.
- **⇅** Headings with a ⇅ can be clicked to expand/collapse that section.

##### Match Report

Frequent players are included in the Select Winner and Select Loser dropdown menus.
- Select the **Winner** name.
- Select the **Loser** name.
- Select the **Match Length**.
- Click on Submit to submit the match report.

After clicking on Submit you can see the submission status at the top of the page.
The Submit button is disabled until the match report submission is Complete.
If another submission is in progress, wait until it is Completed and click Submit again. 
After a new match has been submitted successfully the Rating and Match List will be refreshed automatically.

##### Tournament

The tournament section shows who has to play against who and the summary tournament summary.

Following tournament formats can be played.
- **Double Elimination**: A player must lose two matches to be completely eliminated.
  It provides a "second chance" for players who lose an early match, allowing them to drop from the Main (Undefeated) bracket into a Consolation (Fighters) bracket.
- **Add Last Chance**: Adds a Last Chance bracket to a generated tournament.
- **Round Robin**: Every player plays a match against every other player in the tournament, or within their designated group (pool).
- **Ridgit Robin**: Just a try to implement a round robin where the order in which players play is strictly enforced.
- **Siam Style**: Just a try to implement a tournament style to reduce common issues with other tournament styles, like running out of time.
- **Single Elimination**: The players are paired randomly and the winner of the match advances to the next round, while the loser is immediately eliminated.
- **Consulting Double**: A team-based event where pairs of players compete against other pairs, allowing partners to openly discuss checker plays and doubling cube decisions.

Note that some of the formats are still a work in progress.

The matches are marked with following symbols...
- Past matches are marked with a . Past matches can be hidden by unchecking the Past checkbox below.
- Current matches which are to play are marked with a .
- Future matches which are not yet determined are marked with a . Future matches can be hidden by unchecking the Future checkbox below.

###### Tournament Director

The tournament director section is used by the tournament director to generate and run a tournament.
Information about how to run a tournament can be found in the Tournament Management section.

To run a tournament do the following...
- Set the **Maximum Tournament Players** so that the tournament gets split into two groups if there are too many players.
- Set the **Match Lengthss** where each number is for each round.
- Selected the **Type** of the tournament.
- Select the **Tournament Players**.
- Click **Generate** and review the generated tournament.
- Click **Start** to upload the tournament.
- Click **Finish** to keep the tournament until the next tournament starts.

Note: If the **Forfeit Submitted Matches** checkbox is checked the submitted matches are not enter in the rating list.
Forfeit matches are just shown on your device so that the tournament can be uploaded.

##### Ranking Lists

The different ranking lists are summarized over a specific period in time. 
All ranking lists are shown as a chart in the Ranking Chart section and in tabular form in the Ranking Summary section.

 You can click on the legend entries of a chart to show/hide individual data series.

Which ranking list for which period is shown can be selected in the dropdown menus.
- The **Ranking List** can be selected in the first dropdown menu.
- The **General interval**, Daily, Monthly, Yearly, and ET (Eternally) can be selected in the second dropdown menu.
- The **Specific Period** can be selected in the third dropdown menu.
- The **Player** dropdown menu is only shown for the Player Info ranking list.

- **Rating List**: Shows the current and future rating of the players active in the selected period.
  Press ︎ to replay the selected period a number of times.
  Press ︎ to go back to the current rating.
- **Winning %**: Shows the percent of matches won for each player and the expected percent to win based on the player's Elo points.
- **Rangliste**: Shows the Punkte calculated according to a system based on the German ranking.
  Only matches played on Saturday are considered.
  If a player wins a match the match length is added to the Punkte.
  The Punkte are increased by 5 for each Saturday the player played.
- **Player Progress**: Shows the rating progress over time of the active players in the selected period.
- **Player Position**: Shows the players in an Elo chart based on their actual position.
  The middle part is expanded so that the names do not overlap too much.
- **High/Low Scores**: Shows the high/low scores during the selected period and the current Elo points.
- **Matches Played**: Shows the total matches played.
- **% Matches Won**: Shows the percentage of matches won.
- **Player Info**: Shows how a player is doing against other players.
  The name of the player under consideration is written in the top left.
  The matches won by the other player are written close to that player's name.
- **Current Streak**: Shows how many matches have been won or lost in a row at the end of the selected period.
  Matches Won is indicated by a positive number and Matches Lost is indicated by a negative number.
- **&gt; Winning Streak**: Shows the longest streak of matches won in a row during the selected period.
- **&gt; Losing Streak**: Shows the longest streak of matches lost in a row during the selected period.
- **Days Inactive**: Shows the number of days a player has been idling and the date when the player has played the last time.
  The Days Inactive list makes sense only when the current or recent period is selected.

##### Settings

The settings are saved locally on your device. 
The settings will be loaded automatically the next time you visit or refresh the page. 
Refresh the page after making changes.

- **Your Name**: Enter your name or nickname as it is spelled in the rating list.
  Your name will be highlighted in various lists and your current standing shown in certain charts.
  Further, it has the effect that you will be selected in the Player Info by default.
- **Club**: Select the club you want to see.
- **Theme**: Select any of the choices; System Default, Dark, or Light.

Other settings stored are...
- The state of sections, i.e. if they are expanded or collapsed.
- The selected ranking list.
- The selected interval.
 

### Tournament Management

The tournament director is responsible for the preparation, execution, and closure of the tournament.

##### Preparation
- Collect the entry fee.
- Prepare the scoreboard.
- Set the match length.
- Prepare the pairing tables, if needed.

> Pairing tables are available [here](https://www.3-torens.nl/downloads/Berger%20Tabellen.pdf).

##### Execution
- Run the tournament in an orderly fashion.
- Inform players of their next opponent.
- Make a ruling in case of dispute, if needed ask advice from more experienced players.
- Make sure all results are reported on the scoreboard.
- Inform Modi or Walter in case of wrong reports, like wrong opponent or match length.

##### Closure
- Take a picture of the scoreboard (very important).
- Post the picture in our group.
- Pay out the winners.
- Check if all results have been reported for the rating list.
- Report matches where the opponent forfeited.
- Report missing matches by checking the results on the scoreboard picture.

> Use Matches Played and Daily in the Ranking Lists section to help find out which results are missing. Keep also in mind that some players have played extra non-tournament matches before, during or after the tournament.
